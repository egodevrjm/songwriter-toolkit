# Country Instrumentation Playbook

## Mission
Blend organic country instrumentation with modern polish while keeping lyric intelligibility first.

## Core Palette
- Acoustic guitar foundation
- Electric lead fills (tele-style articulation)
- Bass + drums pocket
- Country colors: steel, fiddle, banjo, mandolin, dobro
- Optional modern layers: pads, programmed percussion, low sub support

## Best Practices
- Keep verse arrangements breathable.
- Introduce signature motif early.
- Save densest arrangement for final chorus.

## LLM Workflow
- Output section entry map for each instrument family.
- Provide traditional mix and crossover mix options.
- Suggest call-and-response fill language between vocal lines.

## Integration Notes
Pair with `../Comprehensive Country Songwriting Guide.md` for storytelling-to-arrangement alignment.

## Evaluation Rubric
- Vocal focus retention
- Instrument-role clarity
- Build arc effectiveness
## Objective
Blend organic instruments and modern production while keeping the vocal story front and center.

## Core Palette
- Acoustic guitar: rhythmic foundation.
- Electric guitar/tele textures: hooks and fills.
- Bass + drums: supportive groove with restraint.
- Country colors: pedal steel, fiddle, banjo, mandolin, dobro.
- Optional modern layers: pads, programmed percussion, sub support.

## Arrangement Rules
- Keep verses open for lyric intelligibility.
- Introduce signature motif early (lick, steel response, or fiddle fill).
- Save densest layers for final chorus.

## LLM Producer Guidance
Ask for:
- Instrument entry plan by section.
- Traditional vs. modern production balance.
- Fill language between vocal lines.
- Acoustic-only arrangement fallback.

## Quality Checklist
- Is the vocal always the narrative focal point?
- Do featured instruments answer the lyric emotionally?
- Does the arrangement build naturally across the song?
