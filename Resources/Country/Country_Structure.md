# Country Structure Playbook

## Mission
Create narrative-first structures that keep hooks central and deliver a strong final tag.

## Baseline Form
Intro (4-8) → Verse 1 (8-16) → Chorus (8) → Verse 2 (8-16) → Chorus (8) → Bridge (8) → Final Chorus + Tag.

## Best Practices
- Get to story premise immediately.
- Ensure each section changes knowledge or emotion.
- Reserve strongest line for final chorus/tag if possible.

## LLM Workflow
- Produce beat sheet and bar map together.
- Offer radio cut and writer-round acoustic cut.
- Add bridge alternatives (hopeful vs. devastating).

## Integration Notes
Cross-check section pacing with `../The Ultimate Country Songwriting Handbook – 2025 Edition.md`.

## Evaluation Rubric
- Narrative progression
- Structural efficiency
- Ending impact
## Objective
Deliver narrative-driven songs with clear section purpose and strong chorus returns.

## Typical Form
- Intro: 4–8 bars
- Verse 1: 8–16 bars
- Chorus: 8 bars
- Verse 2: 8–16 bars
- Chorus: 8 bars
- Bridge: 8 bars
- Final chorus + tag: 8–16 bars

## Structural Principles
- Introduce story context immediately.
- Ensure each verse advances plot or emotional perspective.
- Use final tag to deliver memorable closing line.

## LLM Planning Guidance
Request:
- Beat sheet: what each section must reveal.
- Bar counts and rhyme scheme per section.
- Optional radio edit and acoustic session variants.
- Tag-line alternatives for final chorus.

## Quality Checklist
- Does Verse 2 add new meaning rather than repeat Verse 1?
- Is bridge a true turn, not filler?
- Is ending emotionally conclusive?
