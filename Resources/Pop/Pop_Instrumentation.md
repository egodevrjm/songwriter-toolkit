# Pop Instrumentation Playbook

## Mission
Create polished, vocal-forward arrangements with clear frequency ownership.

## Core Stack
- Drums: tight transient design.
- Bass: mono-compatible sub foundation.
- Harmony: piano/guitar/synth bed.
- Signature hook element: lead synth, guitar motif, or vocal chop.
- Vocal support: doubles, harmonies, ad-lib architecture.

## Best Practices
- Introduce elements progressively.
- Keep lead vocal lane clear in 1-4 kHz.
- Use automation as macro storytelling (width, filter, ambience).

## LLM Workflow
- Output instrument entry map by section.
- Provide register ownership chart.
- Suggest subtractive mix plan: what to mute first when crowded.

## Evaluation Rubric
- Vocal clarity under full chorus stack
- Hook timbre uniqueness
- Translation on earbuds + phone speaker
## Objective
Build clean, modern arrangements where every layer has a clear frequency and rhythmic role.

## Core Palette
- Drums: punchy kick/snare with controlled transient shape.
- Bass: mono-compatible low end with stable sub.
- Harmonic bed: piano, guitar, or synth pad.
- Hook layer: lead synth, vocal chop, or signature guitar motif.
- Vocals: stacked doubles/harmonies for chorus lift.

## Arrangement Rules
- Add elements progressively; avoid full-density intros.
- Leave low-mid space for lead vocal intelligibility.
- Use ear-candy (fills, reverses, throws) sparingly at transitions.

## LLM Producer Guidance
Ask for:
- Instrument list by section.
- Register map (low/mid/high ownership).
- Automation cues (filter opens, reverb throws, mutes).
- “If busy, remove X first” simplification advice.

## Quality Checklist
- Can each part be muted without collapsing the song?
- Is the hook timbre unique within first 30 seconds?
- Are chorus layers denser but still intelligible?
